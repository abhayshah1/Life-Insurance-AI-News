Regulatory & Risk

Colorado AI law remains directly relevant to insurance decisioning controls

Organization: Colorado General Assembly Date: law in force during the July 5, 2026 scan High: primary source

Sourced facts: The bill summary says developers and deployers of high-risk AI systems must use reasonable care to protect consumers from algorithmic discrimination, complete impact assessments, maintain risk management programs, provide notice when AI is a substantial factor in consequential decisions, and support a human-review appeal path where technically feasible. The summary also says insurers or AI developers used by insurers are treated as compliant when subject to Colorado's existing insurance laws on external consumer data, algorithms, and predictive models.

Inference: Life insurers with Colorado exposure should treat AI-enabled underwriting, eligibility, pricing, and servicing as governance-sensitive even if no new enforcement action has surfaced this week.
